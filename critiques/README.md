# Critiques — Project Skepsis Manifesto Library

These documents are a systematic, multi-dimensional critique of Google's **Gemini for Science** initiative (announced I/O 2026). They are not blog posts or hot takes — they are an excavation of the epistemic, ontological, methodological, ethical, legal, and structural gaps in what Google calls "the next era of discovery."

The stance is adversarial but not dismissive: Gemini for Science is the most ambitious AI-for-science play yet, and its failures are instructive. Every gap identified here is a design requirement for anything that claims to improve on it.

## Reading Order

The sequence is chronological by dependency — each file assumes familiarity with those before it.

| # | File | Why This Position |
|---|------|-------------------|
| 01 | `01_overview.md` | Establishes what was announced — the factual grounding for every critique that follows |
| 02 | `02_philosophy.md` | Excavates the philosophical assumptions (epistemology, ontology, ethics, politics) shared by all three tools |
| 03 | `03_structural_triad.md` | Analyzes the architectural logic of the three-tool framework itself — how they relate and what's absent structurally |
| 04 | `04_co_scientist.md` | Deep critique of Hypothesis Generation (Co-Scientist) — the epistemic, methodological, and technical gaps |
| 05 | `05_alphaevolve_era.md` | Deep critique of Computational Discovery (AlphaEvolve + ERA) — optimization-discovery confusion, sandboxing, reproducibility |
| 06 | `06_notebooklm.md` | Deep critique of Literature Insights (NotebookLM) — RAG fragmentation, hermeneutical loss, source-grounding as epistemic prison |
| 07 | `07_science_skills.md` | Critique of the Science Skills bundle — database reductionism, prediction-reality confusion, missing experimental validation bridge |
| 08 | `08_miscellaneous.md` | Cross-cutting gaps: legal/IP, security, governance, economics, workforce, and the final unspoken gap — no theory of own limits |
